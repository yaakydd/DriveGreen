A persistent issue across Africa is the "last mile" problem, where essential medications (vaccines, malaria drugs) often expire or run out in rural clinics due to inefficient logistics.

üß† Data Science/AI Focus
The project is a logistics and forecasting challenge that optimizes the supply chain to minimize stockouts and wastage.

Data Sources: Integrate several disparate datasets.

Historical: Historical consumption rates at every clinic/hospital.

Predictive: Disease incidence forecasts (e.g., predicted malaria cases based on weather/season).

Logistics: Delivery vehicle routes, road quality data (from mapping services), and cold chain sensor data.

Technique: Reinforcement Learning (RL) or Advanced Time-Series Forecasting.

Develop an RL agent that learns the optimal routing and inventory policies across the supply network to minimize total logistical cost while maintaining a 99% stock avoidance rate for critical items.

Standout Impact: A successful model leads to quantifiable savings in wasted drugs and measurable improvements in public health outcomes (e.g., reduced mortality due to prompt access to vaccines). This demonstrates powerful, practical application of AI to national economic and health efficiency.

üéØ SDG Alignment: SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being)
